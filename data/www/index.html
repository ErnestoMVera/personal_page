About

I'm currently finishing my master's degree in Science from Autonomous University of Baja California (UABC), the title of my thesis is Prediction model analysis for Fitness to drive based on visual behaviors; where I used a smart sensing system which consists of an inertial sensor and a smartphone camera, with on-road measurements from the system I analyzed various regression models to predict an on-road driving score.

In 2022 I graduated with honors from the B.S of Computer Engineer of UABC, where I acquired my taste for computer hardware and electronics as well as learning the fundamentals of computer programming and what makes good software.
I participated in the IEEEXtreme programming contest mentor program for the 2023 edition. Teaching competitive programming skills and algorithm design techniques in C++ to 14 students from Latin America who competed in a 24 hour long competitive programming contest. A team of three of my students came in 4th place in the MinIEEEXtreme programming competition.

All sessions are recorded and can be seen here
I did an intership at the Human Motion Analylisis Lab at Instituto Nacional de Rehabilitacion (INR) via a collaboration project between INR and UABC in 2022.
I redesigned a biofeedback system for gait rehabilitation using inertial sensors, which involved redesigning the app architecture, resulting in a performance, maintainability and scalability improvement of the system, as well as the addition of new features such as the flexibility to use any bluetooth inertial sensor with the app.
